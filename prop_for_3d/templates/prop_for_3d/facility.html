Featuring groundbreaking laser direct energy deposition (DED) technology, the Meltio M450 can process both wire and powder and allows for quick changeover of materials, enabling the development of parts printed with 2 or more materials. It will enable innovative remanufacture and repair processes, in a variety of materials, for a range of industries. The compact and portable Meltio is ideal for small to medium size part fabrication and multi-metal 3D printing research. It will revolutionise the rapid replacement of spare parts and in-situ repairs.
Analysis of the measured force vs. displacement curve (particularly the unloading segment) provides the user with quantitative information regarding the mechanical properties of the sample. Values typically obtained from quasi-static nanoindentation testing are Reduced Modulus and Hardness. However other information such as fracture toughness, stiffness, delamination force, and film thickness can also be obtained. All Hysitron standalone nanoindentation systems are capable of in-situ SPM imaging. Using the same probe to scan a sample surface immediately before and/or after a test allows for precise placement of the test as well as observation of deformation events or sample recovery after the test.
